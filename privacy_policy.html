9. ADVERTISERS

Please note that we show third party advertising in our games and also advertise our Services via third parties. We wanted to provide you with more detail about how we use the information for advertising, both in-game and on other third-party properties.

When you view ads on LBP’s properties

To enable us to show in-game ads we may send your advertising ID, IP-address, and other information to advertisers (or ad networks) to enable them to find appropriate ads to serve to you in our games and on third-party properties. You can control and limit our use of your advertising ID in your device settings. LBP does not control which specific ads are shown in our games (this is controlled by ad networks) but we do blacklist certain categories of ads.

Advertising IDs are used by the ad networks for a number of purposes, for example, to:

  • limit the number of times you see the same ad;
  • ensure they are dealing with humans/with real devices and not automated bots;
  • to serve you with advertisements likely to be relevant to you;
  • other brands buy advertisement placement via an ad network or ad exchange to advertise their products in LBP’s games. The ad network or ad exchange acts as an intermediary between the other game companies and LBP. In order for an ad network to charge the company buying the ad, clicks from the ad, and potentially installs of other developers’ applications party apps are tracked. Views, clicks, installs, and similar events from ads are measured using Advertiser ID.

IP-address is typically used by advertisers to broadly see the general geographic area you are coming from for example country, state, and sometimes at the city level.

When you view LBP’s ads on third party services

Little Bit Publishing also advertises its games in other apps by asking ad networks to serve its ads to audiences that will be interested in them. The advertising IDs are used by the ad networks for a number of purposes, including, for example, to measure ad effectiveness (looking at aggregated views, clicks and installs it generates through its advertising in other publishers’ games). Advertising IDs are also used to track views, clicks, installs, and other similar events from ads.

You can opt-out of interest-based advertising on mobile applications by changing the privacy settings of your device and selecting “limit ad tracking” (Apple iOS) or “opt-out of interest-based ads” (Android).
